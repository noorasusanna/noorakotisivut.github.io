Westeros – The world's most exciting continent
Westeros is one of the four known continents in the known world, the others being Essos, Sothoryos, and Ulthos. Most of the area of Westeros is covered by a political entity known as the Seven Kingdoms, while the far north beyond the Wall includes the free folk. The closest foreign nations to Westeros are the Free Cities, a collection of independent city-states across the narrow sea in western Essos. To the south of Westeros lie the Summer Isles. Read more facts about Westeros below.
Facts about Westeros
Westeros Exhibits Bizarre Seasonal Patterns

Westeros is best known for its seasonal variations — the climate of this continent ranges from burning summers to glacial winters and everything in between. The city of Oldtown exhibits tropical weather patterns, King's Landing has a Mediterranean climate, whereas the Storm Lands are almost always rainy. Summers and winters are extremely prolonged on this continent, but these drastic time periods could theoretically be a side-effect of the Night King's magic. The Maesters of the Citadel determine the duration of each season by employing a complex series of geophysical calculations.

The Seven Kingdoms Of Westeros Have Distinct Cultures

Westeros was roughly divided into seven independent Kingdoms before Aegon's Conquest — the North, the Rock, the Isles and the Rivers, the Reach, the Mountain and Vale, the Stormlands, and the Principality of Dorne. That said, the continent has since undergone a plethora of political changes. Westeros now consists of nine distinct territories, namely the North, the Iron Islands, the Vale of Arryn, the Westerlands, the Riverlands, the Reach, the Stormlands, the Crownlands, and Dorne. Each region has its own languages, cultures, customs, liturgies, traditions, mythologies, rituals, and population demographics.

The Continent Is Home To A Number Of Unique Animals

Every Dragon in Game of Thrones comes from Essos, but Westeros is home to a number of equally unique animal species. Fans already know about Direwolves, but few are aware of creatures like Aurochs and Lizard-lions. The mythology of the Iron Islands describes a Kraken-like marine monster, which also doubles as House Greyjoy's sigil, but this entity's existence is yet to be confirmed. Other bizarre animals include Shadowcats, Mammoths, and even Unicorns, the latter being goat-based rather than equine. Unfortunately, Westerosi fauna doesn't get half as much attention as it deserves.

The Lands Of Always Winter Are Still Unexplored

Jon Snow states that Westeros "stretches much farther than any man knows, into the Lands of Always Winter where the White Walkers came from during the Long Night." This region was once dense with green forests and thriving meadows, but became a subarctic wasteland upon the creation of the Night King. The Lands of Always Winter are separated from the rest of Westeros by the Wall, constructed by Brandon the Builder during the Age of Heroes. Jon Snow, Tormund Giantsbane, and several Wildling tribes return to the Lands of Always Winter in the Game of Thrones season finale.
